How the Platform Works

The platform is designed to make public grievance reporting simple for citizens while helping government departments resolve issues faster and more efficiently. It uses location intelligence and automated routing to ensure that complaints reach the correct authorities immediately.

1. Complaint Submission

Citizens can submit complaints through the platform using a simple form.

When submitting a complaint, users provide:

A title and description of the issue

Optional photos or supporting evidence

The category of the problem (for example: road damage, garbage collection, water leakage, streetlight outage)

The system automatically captures the user’s precise location (with permission) using device GPS.

This ensures the issue is accurately mapped to the correct ward or jurisdiction within Bengaluru.

2. Intelligent Location Mapping

Once the complaint is submitted, the platform processes the user’s exact geographic location.

Using this location data, the system identifies:

The relevant municipal ward

The responsible government department

Nearby agencies or service teams responsible for resolving the issue

This eliminates the need for citizens to manually determine which department to contact.

3. Automated Complaint Routing

After identifying the responsible authority, the system automatically routes the complaint to the appropriate agency or department.

Examples:

Road damage → Road Infrastructure Department

Garbage collection issue → Solid Waste Management

Water leakage → Water Supply Board

This routing significantly reduces delays caused by manual complaint forwarding.

4. Complaint Tracking and Updates

After submission, users receive a unique complaint ID.

Through the platform, citizens can:

Track the status of their complaint

View progress updates

See resolution notes from the responsible department

Statuses typically include:

Submitted

In Progress

Resolved

Escalated

This creates full transparency in the complaint resolution process.

5. Resolution and Verification

Once the issue is addressed by the responsible agency, the complaint is marked as resolved.

Users can then:

Confirm that the issue was actually fixed

Provide feedback on the resolution quality

If the issue remains unresolved, the complaint can be reopened for further action.

6. Citizen Reward System

To encourage active civic participation, the platform includes a reward-based incentive system.

Users earn points when they submit helpful and valid complaints that lead to successful issue resolution.

Points are awarded based on:

Accuracy of the report

Usefulness of information provided

Confirmation of a successfully resolved issue

7. Redeeming Rewards

Accumulated points can be redeemed for utility bill benefits, such as:

Water bill credits

Electricity bill credits

Other government service incentives

This system encourages citizens to actively participate in improving their city while receiving tangible benefits.

8. Impact

By combining location intelligence, automated routing, transparent tracking, and citizen incentives, the platform aims to:

Improve response time for civic issues

Reduce unresolved complaints

Increase citizen engagement in governance

Provide actionable insights to city authorities

Ultimately, the platform creates a collaborative ecosystem where citizens and government agencies work together to improve urban infrastructure and services.
